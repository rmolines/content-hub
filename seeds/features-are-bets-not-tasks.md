## Raw context

From thesis.md — the distinction between tasks (known outcome, execute and done) and bets (hypothesis that needs validation). /discovery exists to kill bad bets early with explicit investigation cycles, not to document decisions already made. This reframes the entire feature lifecycle as a series of validated bets rather than a backlog of tasks to grind through.

## Why this matters

Most project management tools treat everything as a task — estimate, assign, track. But features are fundamentally uncertain. The article would argue that treating features as bets changes how you invest time: you front-load investigation to reduce risk before committing execution resources (agent compute, context, time). Kill the bet early or execute with confidence.
