## Raw context

From thesis.md — the entire system is designed to answer "where should I look right now?" for one person juggling multiple agents, not "how do we align the team?" Traditional project management is a communication protocol: standups, status updates, Slack threads, alignment meetings. For a solo founder with AI agents, the bottleneck shifts from team alignment to personal attention allocation. The process becomes a way to manage your own cognitive bandwidth across parallel workstreams.

## Why this matters

Reframes what "process" means in the age of AI agents. The article would argue that solo founders don't need less process — they need different process. One optimized for a single human attention bottleneck, not for multi-human coordination. This is a fundamental category shift that most productivity tools haven't caught up to yet.
